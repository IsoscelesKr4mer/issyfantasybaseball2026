🔎 Week 6 Preview — Apr 27–May 3

📚 Storylines to Watch

The Ragans Administration's run continues TylerV is now atop the standings (32-14-4), atop the cumulative power rankings, and just posted back-to-back 9-0 all-play weeks with 67 and 69 cat wins. The roster has no holes. Cal Raleigh is hitting 4 HR weeks at catcher. Aaron Judge, Mike Trout, Nick Kurtz, Kevin McGonigle, and now Ceddanne Rafaela all hit. The rotation runs six deep before Landen Roupp on the bench. Hey Holmes! is the kind of opponent that sometimes catches a hot team flat. The sim has Ragans at 60%. We will see if anything cools this team off.

Buckner has a chance to forget Week 5 ever happened The Buckner Boots went 0-9 all-play with 15 cat wins last week. The worst single-week performance any team has put up in 2026. Now they get Nick-fil-A. The sim still has Buckner at 75%, and the cat probabilities back it up. 9 of 10 categories lean Buckner. The roster (Tucker, Seager, Murakami, Marte, Bregman, Sale, Valdez, Webb) is still loaded. Mario can pile up SB and tries to close the HR gap, but Mason Miller and Helsley do not save you when the lineup is hitting .280 OBP and the rotation is below your opponent's.

Keanu vs Ete Crow is the only real coin flip Sim says Keanu 53%. Expected score 5.0-5.0. Most categories are sitting at 0.47-0.59 either way. Duke Joe just had his worst pitching week of the season (5.37 ERA) and Ete Crow's Nolan McLean is coming off 17 K. Keanu's edge is the bullpen depth (Chapman, Fairbanks) and balanced power. Ete's edge is the SB game (PCA, Marsee) and a deep rotation that includes McLean, Luzardo, Tolle, and Eury Pérez. Whichever lineup gets healthy first wins this matchup. Juan Soto has been on Keanu's bench with no counting stats for two weeks running.

The hidden-strength teams are still favored Allahu Alvarez is 17-29-4 in the standings but went 7-1-1 all-play last week with the most saves and a Christian Walker / Max Muncy power week. Busch Latte is 19-26-5 but led the league in SB and HR despite a 5.58 ERA. The sim has both teams favored this week. Allahu over Rain City at 69% on the strength of league-best closer depth (Muñoz, Domínguez, Varland, Pagán) facing a Rain City bullpen that has thrown for the wrong team for two months. Busch over OBT at 67%, with Rose's lineup grading out the strongest of any underperforming roster. Two teams whose true strength is not in their record.
