🔎 Week 9 Preview — May 18–May 24

📚 Storylines to Watch

Sonny vs Keanu is the top-2 showdown the calendar was always going to deliver. Cumulative #1 (Sonny, 397 cat wins) meets #2 (Keanu, 400 cat wins). The sim has it 46% Sonny, 4.8–5.2 expected. Keanu just put up 16 HR and 7–0–2 all-play. Sonny just put up 75 K and a 0.93 WHIP. Cole Ragans and Brandon Woodruff still IL for Sonny, Max Fried still IL for Keanu. Closest predicted matchup of the week and the one most likely to flip the cumulative #1 spot.

Hey Holmes! has to prove the upset was real. Michael just rolled 8–1 all-play and 58 cat wins for the best week any team has had this season. Now he gets Ete Crow (3–5–1 last week) as the 38% underdog. Crochet is still IL. Clay Holmes is still IL. The lineup that ran a .345 OBP last week is intact and Arenado is on board for a full week. If Hey wins this, the 10th-place team becomes a real story. If they lose 4–6, it was variance.

OBT catches their first true reset matchup of the year. Three straight weeks beating sim favorites, then a 2–7 H2H L to Hey Holmes! Now Garth gets Nick-fil-A (off a 0–8–1 disaster) as the 60% favorite. Mario’s rotation is Latz, E-Rod, Wrobleski, Davis Martin, Burrows after Bassitt was dropped. Five IL pitchers (Cole, Glasnow, Snell, Helsley, Acuña). Sim sees this as the cleanest win on the board. If it isn’t, the OBT-versus-the-model storyline gets weirder.

Buckner finally favored. Big favorite. Sim has it 72% Buckner over Rain City, expected 5.9–4.1. Webb (IL), Bradley (IL), Strider (IL), Valdez still suspended, and Buckner is still a 72% favorite. The lineup is Murakami, CJ Abrams, Gunnar Henderson, Kyle Tucker, Yandy Díaz, Alex Bregman, Adley Rutschman, Brandon Nimmo. That’s deeper than the prior two weeks suggested. Rain City has Trout (0–0–0–0 last week), Lindor/Anthony/Caballero on IL, and is now leaning on the Skenes / deGrom rotation. Buckner needs this to climb out of the cellar.

Busch vs Allahu is the coin flip nobody is talking about. Sim 47% Busch, expected 4.9–5.1. Both teams sit 8th and 9th in the cumulative cats race and both just lost their H2H games. Busch led the league in SB (12) and OBP (.399) last week. Allahu hit .372 OBP and 39 R. The SV cat goes Allahu 84% because Edwin Díaz is still on the 60-day IL and Busch is running Paul Sewald at closer. The score is going to be a half-category off the prediction either way.
