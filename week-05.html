🔎 Week 5 Preview — Apr 20–Apr 26

📚 Storylines to Watch

Keanu Falls From #1, Then Has To Play Without Soto. The cumulative power rankings have been recomputed and after a 4–5 all-play disaster in Week 4 (just 39 cat wins), Keanu is no longer the runaway top team. Cumulatively still #1, but the lead is real money short. And now Juan Soto is on the IL-10 with a calf, and the matchup is Nick-fil-A, who just hung an 8–2 on Good Vibes Only. The sim still gives Duke Joe a 79% win probability on rotation depth alone (Cam Sanchez, Gavin Williams, Andrew Abbott, Drew Rasmussen, Noah Cameron). But Mario gets the OBP edge (58%) and that’s the path. If Keanu drops back-to-back, the season has a story.

Ragans Walks Into Allahu Alvarez With A 9–0 All-Play Tailwind. TylerV just had the most dominant week of the season: 67 cat wins, 9–0 all-play, 8–1 demolition of Busch. Aaron Judge hit 5 HR. Mike Trout hit 5 HR. Now he draws Allahu, who lost Freddie Freeman to the NA list (Personal), and is missing Jackson Holliday (IL-10), Pivetta (IL-15), Steele (IL-60), and Logan Henderson (NA). The K edge is 90% Ragans. The QS edge is 62% Ragans. The only category Allahu wins outright is SV (75%) on the back of Diaz, Domínguez, and Helsley. Sim says Ragans 65%. The only thing keeping this from a blowout is whether TylerR’s closers can pile up enough cheap saves to drag a couple of categories back.

Busch Latte’s Pitching Staff Is Actively On Fire. Rose was already dealing with Strider, Brown, Miller, and Imai on the IL-15. Now George Springer is IL-10 (toe) and Corbin Carroll is DTD. The starters listed: George Kirby, Reynaldo López. That’s it. Two healthy active SPs to start the week. The bench has Misiorowski, Soriano, Bubic, Weathers ready to be activated, but the rotation thinness is real. Drew Rain City as the matchup, where Skenes and deGrom run a 2.14 ERA staff. Sim: Busch 75%. The all-play numbers say Rain City has been the worst hitting team in the league for two weeks running, and Busch has more than enough lineup to run them off the field. The pitching gap should be smaller than it looks because Rain City’s offense will not score enough.

OBTS Just Beat Keanu. Now They Get Ete Crow. Garth pulled the upset of the season at 24% confidence and is now 20–19–1 in the standings. Reward: a matchup with Ete Crow, who also won last week. The sim flips the favorite to Ete (67%) on the strength of an 84% K edge, 69% QS edge, and 90% SB edge. Jace just put up the league’s best RBI total (42) and now draws an OBTS team that lost SB only because they led the entire league with 11 stolen bases against a team that does not run. Two upset winners colliding. One walks out 6–3, one walks out at .500.

Good Vibes Only Plays The Buckner Boots The Week After A 0–9 All-Play Disaster. Michael lost every category to every team in the league last week. 6.72 ERA. 1.60 WHIP. Zero saves. The reward is a Buckner Boots roster that just took 14 HR and 7 QS in a 6–4 win over Allahu. Adley Rutschman just hit IL-10. Mookie Betts is still IL-10 for GVO. The sim gives Buckner 66% on basically every pitching category being flat, and the GVO offense (33 R, 8 HR last week) being the floor of the league. There is no easy game on this side of the bracket for Michael. This is the start of a stretch where GVO either turns the corner or the season ends in May.
