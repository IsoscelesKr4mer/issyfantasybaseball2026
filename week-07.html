🔎 Week 7 Preview — May 4–May 10

📚 Storylines to Watch

Sonny vs Nick-fil-A is the matchup of the season so far. The new #1 in the standings against the team that has been parked in the contender tier all year. Sim gives Sonny an 88% nuke. The lineup that just went 6–3 on Hey Holmes! gets to feast on a Nick-fil-A roster that's missing Cole, Glasnow, and Helsley simultaneously. Mario’s pitching depth was the calling card; now half of it is on the shelf. If Nick-fil-A loses this 6–4 or worse, the gap between #1 and the chasing pack opens for real.

The sim doesn’t believe in One Ball Two Strikes yet. Garth just delivered a 9–1 sweep of Busch with the highest single-week QS total of the year. Sim response: still a 19% dog against Allahu. Skubal IL stings, but OBT’s rotation also runs Yamamoto, Alcantara, Rodón, plus Burns, Yesavage, and Foster Griffin behind them. The model wants more evidence. Garth’s job is to provide it. Two straight beatings of a sim favorite would make him impossible to argue with.

Ete Crow gets the regression test. Jace just put up the most complete week of the season: 61 cat wins, 9–0 all-play, top of the league in five categories. Sim immediately downgrades him to a 34% underdog against Busch Latte, who went 0–9 all-play with the worst HR/SB output in the league. The model thinks Busch’s roster is still the better one. Jace’s job is to make the model look stupid. Hot lineups don’t politely cool just because regression said so.

Buckner’s rotation runs into the buzzsaw. The Buckner Boots have Logan Webb on IL, Framber Valdez suspended, Taj Bradley on IL, and a Week 6 line that already showed 6 HR / 25 RBI from the lineup. They tied Nick-fil-A on the strength of a 2.11 ERA. Now they get Keanu Reeves, who has Sánchez, Abbott, Williams, plus Max Fried and Freddy Peralta lying in wait on bench. Sim gives Keanu the K cat at 100% and QS at 78%. If Michael loses K 8–2, the ratios won’t save him this time.

Hey Holmes! gets the schedule the standings deserved. Sitting last in the league at 23–43–4 and drawing Rain City, who is one tick above and finally has a lineup that doesn’t quit when the rotation goes off. Rain City has injuries piling up (Lindor, Yelich, Roman Anthony, Correa), but the Skenes/deGrom/Imanaga rotation gives them a floor Hey Holmes! doesn’t have. Sim says 23%. Closer to a 50% if Bryce Harper finds the swing.
