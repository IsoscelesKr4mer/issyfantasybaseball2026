🔎 Week 1 Preview — Mar 25–Mar 29

📚 Storylines to Watch

The GVO Pitching Infirmary Is Open for Business. Good Vibes Only enters Week 1 with three SP60s (Burnes, Greene, Schwellenbach) all signed up for elbow surgery and another IL15 (Bieber). The silver lining: every single one of them is IL-stashed, meaning GVO's active rotation of Crochet, Cease, Bradish, Gore, and Ponce is fully intact. They still have a functioning, competitive staff for now. The question is what happens in June when those IL60 guys are supposed to come back and don't. The ceiling just got a lot lower.

Rain City Bombers Drafted a Rotation, Not a Team. Six starting pitchers in the active rotation (Skenes, deGrom, Imanaga, Flaherty, Sasaki, Burrows) plus Wheeler, Lodolo, and Kelly all properly IL-stashed. That is nine rostered SPs. Their closer situation is Cade Smith and Garrett Cleavinger. This is a very specific set of priorities. Taylor built a Ferrari engine and forgot to install seats. Week 1 they will dominate ERA, WHIP, and K. Whether anyone on the offense picks up HR and RBI is a separate question.

Hader and Strider Are Still in Active Slots. Ete Crow has Josh Hader (biceps, IL15) sitting in an active RP slot instead of the IL. Same deal for Busch Latte's Spencer Strider (oblique, IL15) burning an active SP spot. Both managers are either unaware or unbothered. Either way: move them, free the slot, add someone useful. This is Week 1. Free stuff is still available. The window is closing.

Five-Day Week, Five Games Per Player, Fifty Ways to Lose a Category. Week 1 runs March 25 through 29, five days, roughly five games per batter. Short counting-stat weeks like this are chaos: one cold series tanks your HR total and two bad starts blow ERA for the whole week. Pitching categories are more stable. Teams with elite SPs (One Ball, Rain City, Keanu) have a structural edge that bats alone cannot overcome in five days.

Sleeper & Wire Picks

🌟 Sleeper of the Week

Seth Lugo (SP, KC) — Available. After a breakout 2024 where he posted a sub-3.00 ERA with 170+ strikeouts, Lugo is somehow sitting on the waiver wire in this league. He's a legitimate SP2/SP3 every week with consistent K production and a ground-ball profile that keeps ERA manageable. If you have an active rotation slot, this is the priority add of Week 1. Verified free agent as of Opening Day.

📋 Waiver Wire Targets

Bryan Reynolds (OF, PIT) — Available. Consistent .280/.355 hitter who touches every batting category — R, HR, RBI, SB, and OBP. He's the kind of wire pickup that quietly wins you two or three cats a week without anyone noticing until it's too late. Verified FA.

Mitch Keller (SP, PIT) — Available. Keller averaged nearly 200 IP and 180 K over the last two seasons. Pitching for Pittsburgh means no run support, but ERA and K production are real. Stream him or stash him depending on your rotation depth. Verified FA.

Gleyber Torres (2B, DET) — Available. Reunited with a Detroit lineup that actually hits, Torres has 20-HR upside from the 2B position and a respectable OBP floor. If your 2B slot is thin, this is the move. Verified FA.
