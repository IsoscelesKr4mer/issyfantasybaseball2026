🔎 Week 1 Preview

📚 Storylines to Watch

The defending champs open against... themselves? The Ragans Administration (formerly LetsPlayMajorLeagueBaseball — yes, someone in this league finally admitted that name was unsustainable) faces Rain City Bombers in Week 1. The champs reloaded, Rain City made exactly 17 moves all of last year and finished 9th. Taylor hasn't confirmed whether this was zen or just not giving a damn. Either way, the gap here is real.

The Buckner Boots get the ultimate Week 1 gut check. Michael drafted Kyle Tucker at #8, built an elite OBP roster, and stacked closers. The reward is opening week against One Ball Two Strikes — the squad that drafted Skubal, Yamamoto, and Ramirez and finished 2nd last year. Welcome to the league, Kyle Tucker. You're gonna need those home runs early.

Good Vibes Only vs Busch Latte is a vibes-off. One team is named after a feeling. The other is named after a cheap beer. Rose's Busch Latte won the draft lottery at pick #1 and took the swing on whoever they liked. Vibes (Michael, the other one) counter-programmed by loading up OBP. Fun matchup on paper, five days of actual baseball to settle it.

Four days, not seven. Week 1 is only March 25–29 — five days instead of the usual seven. Short week, so don't burn starters early. Teams with deeper closer stashes actually get a leg up since there are fewer SV opportunities for everyone.

😴 Sleeper of the Week
Parker Messick
SP — Buckner Boots (R22)

Messick came out of nowhere last August — called up in the second half of 2025, posted a 2.72 ERA with 38 Ks in 39.2 innings over 7 starts, and quietly looked like a legitimate rotation piece for Cleveland. Michael grabbed him in Round 22 like he was snagging leftovers. If he locks down a rotation spot and the command holds, that's a round-22 pick outperforming half the league's top-10 pitching selections. Sleeper is an understatement.

📋 Waiver Wire Targets
Emerson Hancock SP — SEA

Hancock is in Seattle's Opening Day rotation after Miller went down with oblique tightness in spring. He had 21 strikeouts in just 15 spring innings and his stuff has taken a step forward. Miller may only miss a start or two, so this is a rent-not-buy situation — but if you need an arm for Week 1, he's available and he's pitching.

Brett Baty 3B — NYM

Strong spring (.333/.420/.920 with 3 homers) and locked into everyday 3B for New York. Power upside in a lineup that's going to score runs. If your 3B situation is sketchy, this is a free upgrade sitting on waivers.

Jonathan India 2B

OBP floor in a 10-cat format that rewards plate discipline. Not flashy, won't steal bases, but he hits the ball hard and doesn't give away at-bats. The kind of add that quietly wins you a category.
