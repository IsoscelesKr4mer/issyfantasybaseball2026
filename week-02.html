🔎 Week 2 Preview — Mar 30–Apr 5

📚 Storylines to Watch

Week 2 is a full slate of rematches, and the storylines from last week haven’t resolved yet. Good Vibes Only faces Busch Latte again after getting drubbed 7–2 in Week 1. Three players on 60-day ILs haven’t changed. What HAS changed is that Michael’s active starters know what they’re doing now, and Crochet is a legitimate ace. This one should be tighter than 7–2, but the structural injury problem doesn’t go away just because the calendar flips.

The Buckner Boots are quietly the most dangerous team in this league right now. They went 8–1 in all-play with Henderson, Bregman, and Rutschman posting combined zeros. If those three start contributing even at a mediocre rate, Buckner has the best overall roster top to bottom. Five starters, Devin Williams closing, and a lineup with actual OBP guys. One Ball held them to 6–4 last week. Ete Crow gets them this week. Good luck.

Ete Crow’s closer situation is a real problem and it isn’t getting solved this week. Josh Hader (IL15) and Carlos Estevez (DTD) leave Raisel Iglesias as the primary save option for the defending champion. Jace Allison built a team that dominates strikeouts via Eury Perez, but losing SV two weeks running turns into a real weakness fast. Meanwhile Ray Donovan’s closer trio of Miller, Helsley, and Romano is the best healthy closer corps in the league. If one of those arms goes down, this matchup flips entirely.

Keanu Reeves won Week 1 by the widest margin in the league, but Week 2 is where we find out if that was dominance or scheduling luck. The simulation shows this is closer than the optics suggest. Skenes’n on deez Hoerners has six active IL slots filled with actual patients, but their core lineup of Alvarez, Freeman, and a healthy Witt is one of the scariest in the league. Duke Joe’s rotation depth is real, but when four of TylerR’s closers all have save opportunities, that SV edge compounds. Most interesting matchup of the week.
